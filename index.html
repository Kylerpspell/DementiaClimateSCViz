What is Dementia?

Dementia is a group of symptoms associated with a decline in cognitive function that affects memory, thinking, behavior, and the ability to perform everyday activities. It is a progressive condition that affects a person's ability to function independently and carry out daily tasks.

The most common cause of dementia is Alzheimer's disease, but other causes include vascular dementia, Lewy body dementia, frontotemporal dementia, and Huntington's disease. Dementia can also be caused by other medical conditions, such as Parkinson's disease, brain injuries, or infections.

The symptoms of dementia can vary depending on the cause and the stage of the disease, but common symptoms include memory loss, difficulty with language and communication, poor judgment, disorientation, and changes in mood and behavior. As the disease progresses, a person may also experience difficulty with movement and coordination, and may require assistance with activities of daily living.
